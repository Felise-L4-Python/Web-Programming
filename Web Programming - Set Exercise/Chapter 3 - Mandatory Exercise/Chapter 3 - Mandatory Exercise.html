Claude Monet: Famous Paintings

Woman with a parasol - Madame Monet and her son

The Woman with a parasol was painted to convey the feeling of a casual family outing. The pose and the placement suggests that his wife and son's stroll were interrupted by Monet to capture their likenesses.

© Claude Monet

Sunflowers

The Sunflowers was exhibited at the seventh Impressionist exhibition. The sunflowers for still life were taken from Monet's garden in Vetheuil, and the painting was admired by critics and Vincent Van Gogh. Monet was focused more on the composition as a whole sunflowers and painted them with no sign of decay.

© Claude Monet

Women in the garden

The Women in the Garden had presented immense challenges for Monet, but he overcame them and creating This stunning masterpiece that became a huge achievement as an early plein air work for Monet. The aim of this work was to discover how figures within a landscape could give the impression that air and light moved around them.
